Making the Transition

I am a regulatory attorney making the transition into full stack web development - and I love it!

I have found that that writing code draws upon my experience in the logic-based disciplines of philosophy and law in new and creative ways. I can apply my problem solving and deductive reasoning skills to the process of writing and debugging code, structuring data models and navigating unfamiliar environments.

The key to this transition has been the Galvanize Full Stack Program, where I am building a solid foundation in the programming languages and technology frameworks needed to develop web applications from front to back. In addition to working daily with Javascript, Node, PostgreSQL, Express and Angular, I have learned to evaluate the impact of design patterns, UI/UX concerns, data modeling, integration/deployment tools and agile methodologies on the development process. Group projects have taught me the importance of utilizing version control systems, pair programming and test-driven design to efficiently deliver successful products.

What is next ?

Now I am actively seeking a role as a junior developer in a collaborative environment that fosters growth and where the quality of the code I write matters. Ultimately, my goal is to be the developer who can efficiently deliver the right technology for the problem at hand, because I truly understand the “why” and “how” of the problem and the solution.

Stacked

Never Without A Good Book

Stacked is a front-end only application that allows users to search for a book by author, title or keyword and add it to their personal book "stack." Users can reference their stack when buying, borrowing, or searching for a book to read and can also mark books as “read” or “unread."

  • Date: November 2015
  • Utilizes: Javascript, jQuery, HTML, Bootstrap, CSS, Gulp, Firebase

Trail Mix

Get The Dirt on Your Next Hike

Trail Mix is a mobile-first, full-stack web-application designed to let outdoor enthusiasts access important information about their planned adventures before hitting the trail and then provide valuable feedback for those following in their path. This group project was completed in four days at the culmination of our second quarter in the Galvanize program.

  • Date: January 2016
  • Utilizes: Javascript, Node.js, Express, PostgreSQL/Knex, SASS, Handlebars, Heroku

Chirp Alert

Meet Single Birds In Your Area

Chirp Alert is a mobile app that allows users to search for bird calls heard in their area and match those sounds to what they hear in real life during outdoor experiences. Users can keep an ongoing list of bird "hearings," access additional information about the birds in their area, and "tweet" about the birds they heard via Twitter. This one-week group project was completed at the end of the third quarter of the Galvanize program.

  • Date: February 2016
  • Utilizes: React Native, Node.js, Express, Javascript, MongoDB, Monk

Tri Reddit Clone

An Angular Experiment

Tri Reddit is a front-end experiment that uses Angular JS to clone the functionality of a Reddit-style news feed that supports images, posts, commenting, voting and filtering.

  • Date: January 2016
  • Utilizes: AngularJS, Javascript, Bootstrap, Heroku
